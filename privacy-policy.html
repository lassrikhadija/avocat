Privacy Policy

Compliant with Quebec's Law 25 (Act to modernize legislative provisions respecting the protection of personal information).

⚠ Demonstration site. "Beaumont Law" is a fictional firm created by NEXTIWEB Studio for demonstration purposes. The contact details below are fictitious. This document illustrates the structure of a Law 25–compliant policy.

Last updated: June 15, 2026

1. Privacy officer

In accordance with Law 25, a person responsible for the protection of personal information oversees this policy. You may reach them at : privacy@beaumont-law.ca.

2. Information we collect

  • Data you provide through the forms (name, email, phone, a brief description of your situation).
  • Essential cookies required for the site to function (storing your consent choice).

We use no advertising cookies or third-party tracking (Google Analytics, Meta Pixel, etc.).

3. Purposes

The information is used solely to : answer your request, briefly assess your matter, contact you back, and keep the site working. It is never sold.

4. Consent

By submitting a form, you consent to the collection described above. You may withdraw your consent at any time (see section 8). The cookie consent banner lets you accept or reject non-essential cookies.

5. Cookies

This site uses only strictly necessary cookies (for example, remembering your consent preference). You can reopen your preferences at any time via the "Manage cookies" button at the bottom of every page, or clear cookies from your browser settings.

6. Sharing and privilege

Every exchange with a lawyer is covered by solicitor–client privilege. Your information is not shared with any third party without your consent, except where required by law.

7. Hosting and retention

Data is hosted on servers located in Canada. It is kept only as long as needed to handle your request, then destroyed in accordance with our legal and professional obligations.

8. Your rights (Law 25)

You have the rights of access, correction, withdrawal of consent and portability of your information. To exercise them, write to the privacy officer. You may also file a complaint with the Commission d'accès à l'information du Québec (CAI).

9. Legal notice

Site publisher (demo) : Beaumont Law (fictional firm). Design and hosting of the demo : NEXTIWEB Studio — web design for lawyers. The text, statistics and reviews shown are fictitious and do not constitute legal advice.

10. Changes

This policy may be updated. The last revision date appears at the top of the document.
